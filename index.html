What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Regularly check your bike helmet for visible damage, worn straps, or an outdated manufacturing date, as helmets typically last 5 to 10 years. Replace it promptly if you see cracks, scratches, signs of wear, or after a significant impact. Prioritize safety by adhering to manufacturer guidelines and promptly replacing your helmet when needed.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, conduct a quick inspection: check tires for damage and inflation, test brakes and gears, inspect for rust, examine cables, verify tightness of bolts, and take a short test ride. If uncertain about its condition, seek a professional bike mechanic's opinion for a more thorough assessment to ensure safety on the road.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, prioritize safety by following traffic rules, signaling your intentions, and wearing a properly fitting helmet. Stay visible with reflective gear, obey traffic signals, and use hand signals to communicate turns. Ride in the same direction as traffic, stay alert to your surroundings, and avoid distractions like phones. Maintain your bike in good condition, yield to pedestrians, and be cautious at intersections. Adhering to these rules enhances your safety and promotes responsible cycling.
